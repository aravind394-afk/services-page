What Is a Humidity Chamber? Uses and Applications

Learn what a Humidity Chamber is, how it works, its common uses, key specifications and the details to check before requesting a quotation.

Written by Hasthas Technical TeamTechnical review: Hasthas Scientific InstrumentsLast reviewed 18 July 2026
Quick answer

Controlled humidity and temperature chamber for environmental testing applications. It is commonly selected for temperature-and-humidity conditioning, stability studies and environmental testing of materials, packaging and laboratory samples. The listed Hasthas model is HSI-125.

What is a Humidity Chamber?

Controlled humidity and temperature chamber for environmental testing applications.

Temperature-and-humidity conditioning, stability studies and environmental testing of materials, packaging and laboratory samples.

How does a Humidity Chamber work?

The chamber combines controlled heating, moisture generation, sensing and circulation to maintain selected temperature and relative-humidity conditions.

Common laboratory uses

Temperature-and-humidity conditioning, stability studies and environmental testing of materials, packaging and laboratory samples.

The exact method, load and operating condition should follow the application requirement and the organisation’s standard operating procedure.

Key specifications at a glance

  • 35%-100% controller range
  • 40%-95% operating humidity
  • Ambient +5°C to 60°C
  • Construction: Double-walled chamber with stainless-steel interior, powder-coated mild-steel exterior, glass-wool insulation and toughened-glass viewing window.
  • Humidity control: Humidistat range 35% to 100%; the catalogue states 40% to 95% maintained humidity with +/- 3% sensitivity.
  • Temperature control: Digital controller from ambient +5°C to 60°C with +/- 1°C accuracy.
  • Heating and cooling: Rear heater and blower for above-ambient operation; evaporator coils, compressor, condenser and fan motor for below-ambient operation.
  • Water system: Water reservoir with automatic low-level cut-off device and alarm.

Available sizes, models and options

  • 605 x 605 x 910 mm
