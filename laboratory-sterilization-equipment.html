Selection Guide

How to select laboratory sterilization equipment

  1. Confirm the material or load that must be sterilized and the normal batch quantity.
  2. Compare chamber diameter, depth, usable capacity, working pressure and electrical load.
  3. Check the lid-locking arrangement, pressure gauge, safety valve, steam release and low-water protection required for the application.
  4. Ask whether a digital controller, timer, automatic pressure control or other optional configuration is needed.
  5. Plan installation space, electrical supply, water handling, operator training and preventive maintenance before ordering.

Common applications

  • College and university laboratories
  • Hospital and medical laboratories
  • Microbiology and biotechnology work
  • Research and development laboratories
  • Industrial testing and quality control

Final equipment selection should be based on the laboratory procedure, applicable safety requirements and the exact operating specification.
