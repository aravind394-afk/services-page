Incubation & Environmental Control

Laboratory Incubators and Controlled Chambers

Laboratory incubators and controlled chambers create stable conditions for microbiology, BOD analysis, preservation, culture work, environmental studies and product testing. The correct equipment depends on the required temperature range, chamber capacity, circulation, humidity or gas-control requirement and the sensitivity of the samples. Hasthas provides several incubator and chamber types for educational, healthcare, research and industrial laboratories.

Selection Guide

How to choose an incubator or chamber

  1. Define the minimum and maximum operating temperature and whether cooling is required.
  2. Match chamber capacity and shelf arrangement to the number and size of samples used each day.
  3. Compare controller accuracy, temperature uniformity, air circulation and recovery after door opening.
  4. For specialised work, confirm humidity control, shaking movement, CO2 control or hot-and-cold cycling requirements.
  5. Check electrical supply, installation clearance, data recording needs and optional timer or stabilizer requirements.

Common applications

  • BOD testing and water analysis
  • Microbiology and culture work
  • Pharmaceutical and healthcare laboratories
  • Environmental and stability testing
  • Research, teaching and industrial quality control

Final equipment selection should be based on the laboratory procedure, applicable safety requirements and the exact operating specification.
