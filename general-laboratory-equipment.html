Selection Guide

How to select laboratory essentials

  1. Start with the exact laboratory process, protection requirement, sample quantity and daily operating time.
  2. For clean-air equipment, identify whether product protection, operator protection, vapour capture or biological containment is required.
  3. For heating and mixing equipment, compare capacity, temperature range, speed control, vessel size and electrical rating.
  4. Check construction materials, controller type, accessories, installation clearance and ventilation requirements.
  5. Share the application and expected workload so the closest suitable model can be recommended.

Common applications

  • Laminar-flow and containment work
  • Fume and vapour extraction
  • Routine heating and sample preparation
  • Stirring, flocculation and digestion
  • Laboratory water distillation

Final equipment selection should be based on the laboratory procedure, applicable safety requirements and the exact operating specification.
