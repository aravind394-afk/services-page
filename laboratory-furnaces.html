Selection Guide

How to select a laboratory furnace

  1. Identify the process, including ashing, heat treatment, high-temperature testing or tubular processing.
  2. Confirm the maximum temperature and the temperature used most frequently.
  3. Compare chamber or tube size, insulation, heating-element design and controller type.
  4. Check the required atmosphere, loading method, electrical rating and duty cycle.
  5. Review ventilation, installation clearance, operator protection and maintenance access.

Common applications

  • Ashing and loss-on-ignition work
  • High-temperature material testing
  • Heat treatment and sample preparation
  • Tubular furnace research applications
  • Industrial quality-control processes

Final equipment selection should be based on the laboratory procedure, applicable safety requirements and the exact operating specification.
