Temperature-Controlled Baths

Laboratory Water Baths and Temperature Baths

Temperature-controlled baths provide stable and uniform heating or cooling around samples and vessels. Laboratories use different bath designs for incubation, serology, viscosity testing, sample warming and controlled-temperature procedures. The right choice depends on operating range, bath capacity, controller accuracy, circulation, vessel size and whether low-temperature operation is required.

Selection Guide

How to choose a laboratory bath

  1. Define the operating temperature range and the required control accuracy.
  2. Measure the vessels, racks or viscometers that must fit inside the working area.
  3. Compare bath capacity, construction material, heater rating, circulation and controller type.
  4. Confirm whether serological racks, viscosity holders, refrigeration or continuous operation are required.
  5. Ask about drainage, water quality, cleaning, over-temperature protection and installation power.

Common applications

  • Serology and sample warming
  • Viscosity and petroleum testing
  • Research and educational laboratories
  • Quality control and process laboratories
  • Low-temperature and constant-temperature procedures

Final equipment selection should be based on the laboratory procedure, applicable safety requirements and the exact operating specification.
